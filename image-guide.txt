----------------------------------------------------------------
  GENERAL TIPS FOR AUTOMOTIVE PHOTOGRAPHY IMAGES
----------------------------------------------------------------

  TIMING
  - Shoot during golden hour (30–60 min after sunrise or before
    sunset) for warm, cinematic light with long shadows.
  - Overcast days produce soft, even light ideal for detail shots.
  - Avoid midday harsh sun — it creates blown-out highlights on
    metallic paint and glass.

  PREPARATION
  - Always photograph a freshly washed and detailed car.
  - Remove personal items from the interior before shooting.
  - Use a clay bar and quick detail spray for a showroom finish.
  - Dress the tyres with tyre shine for a polished look.
  - Straighten the steering wheel for exterior 3/4 shots.

  COMPOSITION
  - Use the rule of thirds — don't always centre the car.
  - Shoot low and wide for an aggressive, cinematic feel.
  - Include environmental context (urban architecture, mountain
    roads, industrial backdrops) to tell a story.
  - Use leading lines (roads, kerbs, railway tracks) to draw the
    eye toward the car.

  EDITING
  - Export final images as JPG at 85–90% quality for web use.
  - Use PNG only for images requiring transparency.
  - Compress all web images using squoosh.app (free) or
    tinyjpg.com before uploading.
  - Target file sizes: hero images under 400KB, gallery images
    under 200KB each.
  - Maintain consistent colour grading across all pages for a
    cohesive brand look.

----------------------------------------------------------------
  COMPRESSION TOOLS
----------------------------------------------------------------

  squoosh.app        — Free web tool, browser-based, excellent
                       for JPG compression with quality preview
  tinyjpg.com        — Batch compress JPG and PNG files online
  tinypng.com        — Specifically for PNG compression
  imageoptim.com     — Mac desktop app for batch optimization
  Lightroom Export   — Use "Quality: 80, sRGB, Resize to Fit"
                       for consistent web-ready exports from raw files
